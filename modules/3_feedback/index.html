Feedback

Feedback is a key part of systems. There are two primary kinds, balancing feedback loops and reinforcing feedback looks.

When you start exercising, it will be really hard to keep going at first. You'll want to quit and go back to your old routine of watching netflix instead. This resistance to change is a balancing feedback loop. At first, the more you exercise, more you'll want to quit and go back to your unhealthy habits.

In order to overcome a balancing feedback loop that keeps you where you are, you'll need a reinforcing feedback loop. This is when doing a behavior causes you to do that behavior more. The feedback loop for exercise is the improvement in healh and performance. As you see yourself improving, you feel more motivated to improve even more.

The stronger the feedback loop, the harder it is to alter it. And reinforcing feedback loops are always temporary. Eventually, you'll platau to a new 'normal' where another balancing loop will resist change. For running this could be a certian distance or frequency.
