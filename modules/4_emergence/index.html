Emergence

The key insight of systems thinking is that certian behaviors or qualities emerge from the interactions between parts.

Your health is not determined by adding up the different behaviors that affect it, it's the outcome of the different parts interacting

When the system itself has a purpose that leads to the component parts behaving differently, it is called emergent. Most of the time, one of the purposes of emergence is to ensure the system's perpetuity into the future. So if you start out being unhealthy, your sleep, diet, exercise, and stress will likely all be interconnected in a way that prevents you from improving your health

If you are able to make a big enough shift to the system, then a new purpose, like 'staying health' could emerge and all the parts will contribute to that new purpose.

Overall, what this means is that systems can be really hard to predict and change. But if a change occurs, it can have rippling effects that lead to changes in behavior throughout the system
